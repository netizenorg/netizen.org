Participate

create, remix, repeat

at the core of any demoparty is virtuosity within restriction. in the old-skool days, crews had very limited machines and often pushed them to the limits to create audio and visual effects that are taken for granted today. many artists use constraints to foster creativity. BrowserFest's contemporary take on the hardware constraints of yore, includes a few rules--outlined below.

BrowserFest rulez

no frameworks. no limits.

As has been mentioned elesewhere on on this site, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript are what the web is built on... wwwhile beautiful and complex frameworks have been built on top of these cornerstones of the web, they tribalize creators and even contributed to a serial dating type relationship with web technologies. (an article from stackoverflow points out that most small frameworks popularity subsides after a few years, w/ larger framworks being fashionable for around a decade). We’re excited return to basics (no, not that BASIC) and create a FUN space to return to the FUNdimentals, share and remix code, and all be on the same page—literally, one page, your demo...

Browserfest Demos will be single HTML pages (with vanilla CSS and JavaScript), submitted through netnet.studio
Any about text, instructions for review or remix should be commented in the code.
Any attribution for media, code, and labor should be commented in the code (or included as shoutouts in the demo)
Demos should be something you're proud of and want to share with the community and wwworld. There is no limit to how many demos you can submit.

in addition to these rules, all participants must adhere to the BrowserFest code of conduct. Our ultimate goal is to create a fun, experimental, and welcoming non-space-space for folx to play with web technologies (and win prizes)!

collaboration

there are a fewww ways u can collaborate with others in BrowserFest. traditionally, demo crews have many different roles. for exe.ample, there are often visual artist, musicians, txt writers, and coders that all contribute bits to a demo. if you already have a crew, great! if you're looking for others to collaborate, we'll be launching a discord in the first week of the fest where you can meet others looking to team up.

if u prefer to fly solo, remixing may be up ur alley. remixes and mashups are at the heart of creating stuff online--from code to memes. Browserfest embraces this and is building the ability to remix demos into the submission process (stay tuned). you can immediatly start informatlly remixing by checking out the examples we've curated w/in netnet.

how do i win?

there are 3 types of awards: Judges Awards, Community Awards, and WildCard Awards. all demos will be judged on

Judges will Award demos based on attribution and call-outs, technical creatitivy, unconventional uses of HTML, CSS, and JS, poetics, and personal taste.
Community awards will be driven by the system. These will include most remixed demo, best demo remix evolution, and more
WildCard Awards are special awards that will go to demos that break the mould and honor the scene.

Examples of WildCard Awards may include: Best Music/Audio, Weirdest Browser Feature, Most Skeuomorphic, and no-div. These are not set in stone and may update at any time. Heck, if you submit something genera-bending or left-field, a yet-to-be-created category may out there for ya!
